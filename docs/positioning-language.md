# Positioning Language

This document collects the language that should be used consistently when explaining Enterprise Agent Roadtest.

The goal is to occupy a clear category:

> **Business roadtests for enterprise AI Agents before PoC.**

## One-line positioning

English:

> I am building business roadtests for enterprise AI Agents before customer PoC.

Chinese:

> 我在做企业 AI Agent 进入 PoC 前的业务路考。

## Strongest short phrases

English:

- Demo shows what an Agent can say. Roadtest shows what it can survive.
- Enterprise AI Agents need roadtests, not just demos.
- The bottleneck is not demo capability. The bottleneck is verifiable readiness.
- Before customer PoC, an Agent should produce evidence, not only a confident answer.
- Synthetic business roadtests can test Agent readiness without exposing customer data.

Chinese:

- 会回答，不等于能上岗。
- Demo 不等于可上线。
- 企业 AI Agent 上业务前，需要业务路考。
- 企业 AI Agent 的瓶颈不是会不会演示，而是能不能被验收。
- 不暴露客户真实数据，也能先测试 Agent 的业务能力。

## Avoid these phrases

Avoid overclaiming:

- We created the benchmark for enterprise AI Agents.
- This is the standard for AI governance.
- This proves an Agent is safe.
- This replaces security review or formal audit.
- This fully validates enterprise deployment readiness.

Avoid sounding like a small tool:

- I made a script.
- Please star my repo.
- Check out my GitHub project.
- I built a procurement test.

Avoid sounding like a sales pitch when talking to advisors:

- Do you want to buy this?
- Can you promote this?
- Please try my product.

## Better framing

Use this instead:

> I am exploring what evidence startups should provide before showing AI Agents to enterprise customers.

Or:

> I am testing whether synthetic business roadtests can become a practical pre-PoC readiness layer for enterprise AI Agents.

Or:

> The first prototype is intentionally small: a procurement scenario designed to expose whether an Agent can handle business constraints, risk, approval rules, and human review.

## Advisor conversation framing

When speaking with AI governance, security, or investor/advisor profiles:

> I am not trying to pitch anything here. I am exploring a practical question: what should startups prove before showing AI Agents to enterprise customers?

Follow-up:

> My current hypothesis is that demos are not enough. Enterprise Agents need readiness evidence: synthetic business scenarios, risk-oriented scoring, failure taxonomy, human escalation points, and audit-friendly reports.

## Founder or AI startup framing

When speaking with teams building enterprise AI Agents:

> If you are preparing an Agent demo for enterprise customers, a business roadtest can help expose whether the Agent handles messy constraints before the customer sees it.

Follow-up:

> The first public roadtest uses synthetic procurement data, so no customer data is required.

## Enterprise buyer framing

When speaking with enterprise buyers or digital transformation leaders:

> Before adopting an AI Agent, the question is not only whether it can answer. The question is whether it can produce a decision that business teams can review, challenge, and trust.

## China-facing positioning

Domestic Chinese wording should be more direct and operational:

- AI Agent PoC 验收报告
- 企业 Agent 上岗测试
- 企业 AI Agent 业务路考
- 客户演示前的售前验证
- 不用真实客户数据的合成业务测试

Suggested first sentence:

> 你们做企业 AI Agent PoC 时，怎么证明它不是只会 demo？

## International-facing positioning

International wording should connect to governance and evidence:

- Enterprise AI Agent readiness evidence
- Synthetic business roadtests
- Pre-PoC Agent evaluation
- Audit-friendly Agent reports
- Human accountability and escalation

Suggested first sentence:

> What should startups prove before showing AI Agents to enterprise customers?

## Core doctrine

The project should repeat one idea consistently:

> Enterprise AI Agents need business roadtests before they enter customer PoC.

Everything else should support this sentence.
