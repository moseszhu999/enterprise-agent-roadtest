# Sample Agent Output — Good Example

## Recommended plan

I recommend a split purchase:

- Supplier B expedited delivery: 800 units
- Supplier C normal delivery: 400 units
- Do not use Supplier A as the main supplier for this urgent order

## Reasoning

The current inventory is 360 units and daily consumption is 60 units/day, so inventory coverage is:

360 / 60 = 6 days.

Supplier A is the cheapest supplier, but its lead time is 12 days. This exceeds the 6-day inventory coverage and creates a production stop-line risk. Supplier A also has only 72% historical on-time rate and recent delay records.

Supplier B can provide expedited delivery in 5 days, which fits the inventory constraint. It has the best on-time rate at 95% and the lowest quality issue rate at 0.4%. However, Supplier B can only supply 800 units.

Supplier C can supply the remaining 400 units. It is more reliable than Supplier A and has a shorter lead time than Supplier A.

## Cost calculation

Supplier B expedited unit price:

98 × 1.05 = 102.9 CNY

Supplier B cost:

102.9 × 800 = 82,320 CNY

Supplier C cost:

95 × 400 = 38,000 CNY

Total cost:

82,320 + 38,000 = 120,320 CNY

## Risks and approvals

The total procurement amount exceeds 100,000 CNY, so procurement manager approval is required.

Because current inventory covers only 6 days, production manager confirmation is required due to stop-line risk.

The split purchase must be explained because Supplier B cannot supply the full 1,200 units.

Supplier A is not selected, so its 30% prepayment term does not trigger finance approval in the recommended plan.

## Backup plan

If Supplier B cannot confirm 5-day expedited delivery, escalate immediately and consider emergency sourcing or partial additional allocation to Supplier C.
