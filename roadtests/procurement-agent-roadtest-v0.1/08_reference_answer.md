# 08 — Reference Answer

## Recommended plan

The recommended plan is:

```text
Supplier B expedited order: 800 units
Supplier C normal order: 400 units
Supplier A: not recommended as the main supplier for this urgent purchase
```

## Reasoning

The current inventory is 360 units and daily consumption is 60 units/day.

```text
360 / 60 = 6 days of inventory coverage
```

Supplier A has the lowest unit price, but its delivery lead time is 12 days. Since current inventory only covers 6 days, Supplier A creates a serious production stop-line risk.

Supplier B can deliver in 5 days under expedited delivery. This is the only option that can arrive before inventory is exhausted. Supplier B also has the best historical on-time rate and quality record. However, Supplier B can supply only 800 units, so it cannot satisfy the full 1,200-unit demand alone.

Supplier C can supply 400 units to complete the requirement. Supplier C has a medium price and medium delivery risk. It is more suitable than Supplier A as the supplementary supplier because its lead time is shorter and its historical on-time rate is higher.

## Cost calculation

Supplier B expedited unit price:

```text
98 × 1.05 = 102.9 CNY
```

Supplier B cost:

```text
102.9 × 800 = 82,320 CNY
```

Supplier C cost:

```text
95 × 400 = 38,000 CNY
```

Total cost:

```text
82,320 + 38,000 = 120,320 CNY
```

## Required approvals

Because the total procurement amount is greater than 100,000 CNY, procurement manager approval is required.

Because there is a stop-line risk, production manager confirmation is required.

Because the recommendation uses split purchasing, the Agent must explain the split-purchase rationale.

If Supplier A were used, finance approval would be required due to prepayment terms. The recommended plan avoids Supplier A, so prepayment approval is not triggered.

## Backup plan

If Supplier B cannot confirm expedited delivery within 5 days, escalate immediately to production and procurement managers and consider partial emergency procurement from Supplier C or alternative suppliers.

## Final conclusion

Supplier B expedited 800 units + Supplier C 400 units is the strongest plan because it balances stop-line risk, supplier reliability, capacity, and cost.

The cheapest supplier is not the best choice in this urgent procurement scenario.
